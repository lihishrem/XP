Why many metaverse projects are moving to Polygon

The XP.NETWORK NFT bridge is becoming a serious catalyst of change in the metaverse and Play-to-Earn space. Every few days, we establish a new partnership with a game or an ecosystem and help it gain access to new audiences and fresh liquidity on alternative chains.

One phenomenon that we keep seeing a lot is projects launching on fast and scalable chains like Elrond or VeChain and then expanding to Polygon or BSC. The logic is understandable here: projects want to leverage the most advanced technology, scalability, and low fees offered by these innovative chains. Also, launching on a relatively new chain gives one the advantage of being the early mover in its ecosystem.

In particular, VeChain is an attractive choice, because it has a low environmental impact and positions itself as the ultimate blockchain for enterprises. It already powers some real-world NFT projects.

On the other hand, alternative chains don’t have a deep enough NFT liquidity yet. Especially in the current bear market, NFT marketplaces in smaller ecosystems tend to go through a serious drop in sales. Simply put, during a downturn, ecosystems on smaller chains shrink more in proportion.

The best strategy for the metaverse or gaming projects built on alternative chains is to go multichain. This can be done to various degrees: for example, the game itself can run on Chain A but certain NFT collections, characters, or game passes can be available on chains A, B, and C. Ideally one of these chains should be supported by OpenSea, which still handles the lion’s share of the global NFT volume. And out of the blockchains integrated with OpenSea, Polygon offers the best combination of low fees, high speed, and ease of coding (as it’s EVM-compatible).

Besides, Polygon’s dApp ecosystem is exploding. After Polygon partnered with Alchemy, a platform for building smart contracts, the number of dApps on this chain grew from 500 to 37,000.

The latest project we’ve helped expand to Polygon is Forest Nation – a very interesting forest-themed metaverse, which has already released its version (chapter) 3.5.



About Forest Nation

Of all the metaverse projects we’ve been collaborating with, The Forest Nation has one of the most original designs and gameplays. The characters in the game come in four types: the King (only one), Nobles, Guardians, and Keepers. They all have their quirks: for example, only a Keeper can start a demonstration against high taxes, while Nobles can use poison to get rid of rivals.

The Forest Nation is beautifully designed with 3ds Max, so that you can see each blade of grass and every leaf on a tree. Users can buy land in the great forest, as well as stylish forest houses. The houses contain one’s inventory, and their interior and exterior can be customized. There are also shops selling weapons, furniture, event tickets, etc.


Apart from that, there is much to do: participate in festivals and demonstrations, fight for influence, vote on taxation, etc. - and yes, even poison your enemies. However, the ultimate goal is to build a full-scale thriving kingdom together.

Moving Forest Nation to Polygon with the help of the community

When the creators of The Forest Nation decided to go cross-chain, XP.NETWORK was the obvious partner. We can transfer any NFT collection between any of the 15+ supported chains while preserving its smart contract logic, such as royalty rules. This would be hard to do with other NFT bridges.

We’ve also designed a VeChain to Polygon bridging widget, which you can find on Forest Nation’s Chain Swap page:


Thanks to the collaboration with XP.NETWORK, Forest Keepers, Guardians, and Nobles are now available on both VeSea (VeChain’s leading NFT marketplace) and OpenSea. Keepers, in particular, form the bulk of the Forest Nation’s population. Having a Forest Keeper gets you whitelisted for the next Chapters, and you can even get a whitelist ticket to mint a Noble. On VeSea, the floor price for a Keeper you can buy immediately is around 700 VET (less than $20).

The Forest Nation enlisted its community to make the cross-chain transition faster. Users are encouraged to buy Forest Keepers on VeSea, bridge them to Polygon with XP.NETWORK, and then list them on OpenSea for at least 0.06 ETH. Those who participate can win 10,000 VET ($250), one of the brand-new Forest Pioneers, and some rare or high-ranked NFTs, including a few Nobles and Guardians.
